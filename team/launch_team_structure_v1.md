# Launch Team Structure v1

This document defines the working structure for the bridal veil launch team. It is designed as a task-routing map: when a new task appears, place it under the right function so ownership stays clear.

## 1. Founder / Brand Owner

**Core responsibility:** Final judgment on brand direction, priorities, budget, and trade-offs.

**Owns:**

- Brand vision and positioning
- Final SKU approval
- Final pricing decisions
- Budget approval
- Hiring and vendor approval
- Major launch timeline decisions
- Investor, partner, and strategic relationship decisions

**Tasks that go here:**

- Decide whether a SKU fits the brand
- Approve first launch assortment
- Choose final price architecture
- Decide whether to spend on content, inventory, or ads first
- Decide brand tone, website direction, and customer promise

## 2. Product / Merchandising / Sourcing

**Core responsibility:** Decide what to sell, how much to buy, from whom, at what cost, and when to reorder.

This is one of the most important functions for this project. For bridal veils, procurement cannot be separated from merchandising because product choice, supplier capability, quality, margin, and inventory risk are connected.

**Owns:**

- SKU architecture
- Launch assortment
- Initial buy plan
- Supplier sourcing
- Sample development
- Cost negotiation
- MOQ negotiation
- Production timeline
- Quality standards
- Reorder rules
- Sell-through review
- Product lifecycle decisions

**Tasks that go here:**

- Find and compare factories
- Request samples
- Evaluate lace, tulle, pearl density, edge finish, comb quality, packaging quality
- Decide initial quantity by SKU
- Decide whether to reorder, pause, or retire a SKU
- Track COGS, landed cost, and gross margin
- Create PO details
- Check whether inventory can support one-week shipping
- Maintain SKU-level inventory rules

**Future role name:** Product & Sourcing Lead, or 商品采购负责人.

## 3. Creative / Content / Editorial

**Core responsibility:** Make the brand visually desirable, trustworthy, and clear to modern brides.

**Owns:**

- Creative direction
- Campaign concept
- Product photography
- Editorial photography
- Short-form video
- UGC briefs
- Creator content direction
- Website visual assets
- Product page imagery
- Social feed look and feel

**Tasks that go here:**

- Plan launch shoot
- Create shot list
- Decide model, styling, location, hair, makeup, gown pairing
- Write TikTok and Reels concepts
- Make Pinterest-friendly visuals
- Create product page image order
- Produce comparison visuals such as cathedral vs chapel, mantilla vs pearl
- Review whether content feels bridal, modern, and trustworthy

## 4. Growth / Marketing

**Core responsibility:** Bring qualified brides to the site and turn traffic into a repeatable growth system.

**Owns:**

- SEO
- Affiliate
- Creator outreach
- Organic social
- Paid search testing
- Referral strategy
- Email/SMS acquisition
- Channel performance tracking
- Launch calendar
- Traffic and conversion assumptions

**Tasks that go here:**

- Build SEO keyword map
- Write or commission blog/content pages
- Find affiliate partners
- Contact creators
- Track traffic sources
- Test paid search keywords
- Build creator seeding list
- Plan launch posts
- Track CAC, conversion rate, AOV, and revenue by channel

## 5. E-commerce / Website / Conversion

**Core responsibility:** Make the website clear, functional, shoppable, and conversion-focused.

**Owns:**

- Shopify setup
- Product pages
- Collections
- Navigation
- Quiz or guide experience
- Checkout flow
- Product filters
- Size/length education
- Analytics tracking
- Conversion rate optimization

**Tasks that go here:**

- Build product detail pages
- Set up Shopify products and variants
- Create collection structure
- Add product comparison guides
- Set up email capture
- Install analytics and tracking
- Improve product page copy
- Make shipping and return policy clear
- Add reviews, FAQs, and trust signals

## 6. Operations / Fulfillment / Customer Experience

**Core responsibility:** Make sure orders, inventory, shipping, returns, and customer communication work smoothly.

**Owns:**

- Inventory receiving
- Storage
- Picking and packing
- Packaging workflow
- Shipping workflow
- Return/exchange handling
- Customer service
- Order issue escalation
- Delivery promise

**Tasks that go here:**

- Receive first inventory
- Check actual inventory against PO
- Pack orders
- Create packing checklist
- Handle damaged product cases
- Reply to customer questions
- Maintain one-week shipping promise
- Track return reasons
- Create customer service templates

## 7. Finance / Analytics

**Core responsibility:** Keep the business financially clear and make decisions based on real numbers.

**Owns:**

- Financial model
- Startup cost tracking
- Cash flow
- Monthly P&L
- SKU profitability
- Inventory cash tied up
- Marketing budget
- Scenario planning
- Performance dashboard

**Tasks that go here:**

- Update financial model
- Track actual spend vs plan
- Calculate gross margin by SKU
- Review monthly revenue and profit
- Decide reorder cash capacity
- Compare base, conservative, and upside scenarios
- Track payback period for marketing spend

## 8. Admin / Legal / People

**Core responsibility:** Keep the business infrastructure clean enough to operate.

**Owns:**

- Business registration
- Contracts
- Vendor agreements
- Trademark tracking
- Basic accounting setup
- File organization
- Internal documentation
- Team access and permissions

**Tasks that go here:**

- Organize GitHub files
- Share documents with collaborators
- Track vendor contracts
- Manage invoices
- Create folder structure
- Keep passwords and credentials out of shared docs
- Maintain decision notes

## Minimum Launch Team

At launch, these do not need to be eight full-time departments. The practical lean team can be:

- Founder / Brand Owner: you
- Product & Sourcing Lead: part-time, consultant, or strong operator
- Creative / Content: freelancer or small studio
- Growth / E-commerce: freelancer, agency, or hybrid operator
- Operations / Customer Experience: part-time assistant or founder-led at first
- Finance / Analytics: founder-led with model support

## How To Route New Work

Use this question:

**What result does this task affect most directly?**

- What are we selling? → Product / Merchandising / Sourcing
- How does it look and feel? → Creative / Content / Editorial
- How do customers find us? → Growth / Marketing
- How do customers buy? → E-commerce / Website / Conversion
- How do we deliver and support? → Operations / Fulfillment / Customer Experience
- How do the numbers work? → Finance / Analytics
- How do we keep the business organized? → Admin / Legal / People

## Important Principle

For this project, the hardest early function is not generic marketing. It is the connection between:

Product choice → content quality → customer trust → traffic conversion → inventory decisions.

The team should be built around that loop.
